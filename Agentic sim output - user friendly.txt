## Simulation Setup: Step-by-Step Breakdown

This simulation models the US stock market's recovery over 8 quarters, with each step representing one quarter. It involves three agent groups: institutional investors, retail investors, and the Federal Reserve. Below, I outline each step in the setup process, including the specific actions and variables added for each agent group.

---

### Step 1: Set Simulation Parameters
- **Action**: `SimulationParameters`
- **Details**:
  - The simulation runs for **8 steps**, where each step represents one **quarter**.
  - This establishes the timeframe to observe medium-term recovery dynamics post-pandemic.

---

### Step 2: Add Institutional Investors
- **Action**: `AddAgentGroup`
- **Details**:
  - **Group Name**: `institutional_investors`
  - **Number of Agents**: 10
  - **Memory Length**: 4 quarters
  - **Role**: Represents large investors (e.g., pension funds, mutual funds) with significant capital and influence on market movements.

---

### Step 3: Add Retail Investors
- **Action**: `AddAgentGroup`
- **Details**:
  - **Group Name**: `retail_investors`
  - **Number of Agents**: 50
  - **Memory Length**: 2 quarters
  - **Role**: Represents individual investors with smaller capital but collective impact on market trends.

---

### Step 4: Add Federal Reserve
- **Action**: `AddAgentGroup`
- **Details**:
  - **Group Name**: `federal_reserve`
  - **Number of Agents**: 1
  - **Memory Length**: 6 quarters
  - **Role**: Influences the market through monetary policy, primarily by setting the interest rate.

---

### Step 5: Add Inflation Rate Variable (Federal Reserve)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `inflation_rate`
  - **Description**: Tracks the current inflation rate (year-over-year percentage change).
  - **Unit**: Percent
  - **Initial Value**: 4.0%
  - **Update Rule**: `self.inflation_rate + (np.random.normal(0, 0.3) + (3 - self.interest_rate) * 0.2 + (0.1 * self.step))`
  - **Visibility**: True (observable by other agents)
  - **Purpose**: Simulates how inflation evolves based on interest rates, random fluctuations, and time progression.

---

### Step 6: Add Interest Rate Variable (Federal Reserve)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `interest_rate`
  - **Description**: Represents the federal funds rate set by the Federal Reserve.
  - **Unit**: Percent
  - **Initial Value**: 0.25%
  - **Update Rule**: `self.interest_rate_decision if self.interest_rate_decision is not None else self.interest_rate`
  - **Visibility**: True
  - **Purpose**: Updated based on the Federal Reserve’s decision each quarter.

---

### Step 7: Add GDP Growth Variable (Federal Reserve)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `gdp_growth`
  - **Description**: Tracks quarterly GDP growth rate (annualized).
  - **Unit**: Percent
  - **Initial Value**: 2.5%
  - **Update Rule**: `max(-2, min(8, 5 - (self.interest_rate * 0.5) + (1 / (self.step + 1) * 3) + np.random.normal(0, 0.8)))`
  - **Visibility**: True
  - **Purpose**: Reflects economic growth, constrained between -2% and 8%, influenced by interest rates and random noise.

---

### Step 8: Add Unemployment Rate Variable (Federal Reserve)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `unemployment_rate`
  - **Description**: Tracks the current unemployment rate.
  - **Unit**: Percent
  - **Initial Value**: 6.0%
  - **Update Rule**: `max(3, min(10, self.unemployment_rate - (self.gdp_growth * 0.15) + (self.interest_rate * 0.1) + np.random.normal(0, 0.2)))`
  - **Visibility**: True
  - **Purpose**: Adjusted based on GDP growth, interest rates, and random fluctuations, bounded between 3% and 10%.

---

### Step 9: Add Interest Rate Decision Action (Federal Reserve)
- **Action**: `AddAction`
- **Details**:
  - **Action Name**: `interest_rate_decision`
  - **Description**: Determines the federal funds rate for the upcoming quarter.
  - **Unit**: Percent
  - **Options**: [0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.25, 1.5, 1.75, 2.0, 2.25, 2.5, 2.75, 3.0, 3.5, 4.0, 4.5, 5.0]
  - **Purpose**: Allows the Federal Reserve to adjust monetary policy each quarter.

---

### Step 10: Add Market Index Variable (Institutional Investors)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `market_index`
  - **Description**: Represents the S&P 500 index level, reflecting overall market performance.
  - **Unit**: Points
  - **Initial Value**: 4200
  - **Update Rule**: `self.market_index * (1 + (federal_reserve.gdp_growth[0] * 0.05 - federal_reserve.interest_rate[0] * 0.03 + 0.01 - federal_reserve.inflation_rate[0] * 0.01 + np.random.normal(0, 0.025) + (np.sum(institutional_investors.investment_decision == "buy") - np.sum(institutional_investors.investment_decision == "sell")) * 0.003 + (np.sum(retail_investors.investment_decision == "buy") - np.sum(retail_investors.investment_decision == "sell")) * 0.0007))`
  - **Visibility**: True
  - **Purpose**: Captures market dynamics driven by economic factors and collective investor actions.

---

### Step 11: Add Risk Appetite Variable (Institutional Investors)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `risk_appetite`
  - **Description**: Measures willingness to take risks (higher values indicate greater risk tolerance).
  - **Unit**: Score
  - **Initial Value**: Uniformly distributed between 3 and 8
  - **Update Rule**: `max(1, min(10, self.risk_appetite + (federal_reserve.gdp_growth[0] * 0.2 - federal_reserve.interest_rate[0] * 0.3 - federal_reserve.inflation_rate[0] * 0.1 + np.random.normal(0, 0.5))))`
  - **Visibility**: True
  - **Purpose**: Adjusts based on economic conditions and random variation, bounded between 1 and 10.

---

### Step 12: Add Investment Capital Variable (Institutional Investors)
- **Action**: `AddVariable`
- **Details**:
  - **Variable Name**: `investment_capital`
  - **Description**: Tracks capital available for investment.
  - **Unit**: Billion USD
  - **Initial Value**: Uniformly distributed between 5 and 50 billion USD
  - **Update Rule**: `self.investment_capital * (1 + ((institutional_investors.market_index[0] / max(1, np.mean([institutional_investors.market_index[0] for i in range(min(4, self.step + 1))])) - 1) * 0.7) + np.random.normal(0, 0.01))`
  - **Visibility**: True
  - **Purpose**: Grows or shrinks based on market performance relative to recent history.

---

### Step 13: Add Investment Decision Action (Institutional Investors)

## Summary of Agent Variables and Actions

### Federal Reserve
- **Variables**:
  - `inflation_rate`: Monitors inflation, updated with interest rate effects and random noise.
  - `interest_rate`: Set by the Fed’s decision, influencing economic variables.
  - `gdp_growth`: Tracks economic growth, affected by interest rates.
  - `unemployment_rate`: Reflects labor market conditions, tied to GDP and interest rates.
- **Action**:
  - `interest_rate_decision`: Adjusts the interest rate each quarter.

### Institutional Investors
- **Variables**:
  - `market_index`: Tracks overall market performance, driven by economic factors and investor actions.
  - `risk_appetite`: Measures risk tolerance, updated by economic conditions.
  - `investment_capital`: Tracks available capital, tied to market performance.
  - `preferred_sector`: Indicates current sector focus, updated by decisions.
  - `tech_performance`, `healthcare_performance`, `financials_performance`: Monitor specific sector trends.
- **Actions**:
  - `investment_decision`: Determines buying, holding, or selling.
  - `sector_rotation_decision`: Shifts investment focus across sectors.

### Retail Investors
- **Variables**:
  - `market_sentiment`: Gauges confidence, influenced by market and economic trends.
  - `disposable_income`: Tracks investable income, adjusted by economic factors.
- **Action**:
  - `investment_decision`: Decides buying, holding, or selling.
